3:35 PM

Bangalore Wadaiko Club

Wadaiko is a traditional Japanese percussion instrument that has been passed down from ancient times. It is one of the most easily understood aspects of Japanese culture, and its powerful sound is very popular across the world. Through their Wadaiko performances, the Bangalore Wadaiko Club hopes to introduce the Japanese cultural art of taiko drumming to as many Indian audiences as possible, and would be grateful if you could watch them and cheer them on!

3:50 PM

Dance performance by Team Clown Fiesta

This dance group is here to take you on a fun journey through the rich and diverse cultural landscape of Japan. From the energetic beats of modern J-pop and anime hits to the soul-stirring rhythms of traditional Soran Bushi, they blend the old and the new with elegance and energy. You'll witness the lively movements of Yosakoi-style dance, where tradition meets modern creativity, and groove to retro-inspired steps that will leave you tapping your feet.

4:05 PM

Bumbu Kan. Okinawa Traditional Kobudo Dojo Okinawa Kobudo 2024 World Champion, Yuki Okajima

Yuki will demonstrate a kata with a weapon called "Sai," with which she won the world tournament in this August. Her students will perform a kata with Bo weapon. Kobudo is, simply put, the “karate with weapons.” Here is Yuki's message: “Be strong. That will make you kind. Be kind. That will make you want to be stronger. Dream. That will stay with you when you are alone. You'll be strong. Love and Light, India."

5:45 PM

Sawagi (Nihon-buyou)

"Sawagi" is a Japanese classical folk song which originated during the Edo period when Yoshiwara was Japan's most bustling entertainment district and cultural center. It spread throughout the country as a traditional Japanese song, and is now performed as a standard dance piece. Ranita will be performing classical Japanese dance on this song using sensu (Japanese fan) as a prop.

Kanapon

Kanapon is a 4' 9" solo idol whose special skill is the robot (dance style).
